1. Flatness

They do not know why and how they got here but for their whole lives, these people have been surviving off bugs, distasteful water, and surrounded by nothing but darkness, being known as cave people. One day, they encounter a strange entity shown on the cave wall. In the eyes of these people, they see a pointy cornered shadow with a smooth top, misinterpreting it for a monster. They are afraid and believe that it is the end of the world for them as they have never encountered anything like this before. Upon seeing this new entity, they believe it is a sign from the gods. Being unsure why this shadow appeared, they worship it as it has given them light and a reason to live within the cave. Sometimes the unusual shadow figure would move. The cave people believe when it moves up on the wall, it means that gods are close to them, calling to them, resulting in them performing a dancing as well as a singing ritual, hoping it would please the gods. When the shadow descends, they believe that they are straying away from the gods, causing them to go into a distraught state. These cave people are constantly going in a repeating cycle of hope and happiness, to an end of the world mindset

Nick Sousanis's concept of “Flatness” refers to how everyone is viewing everything with a lack of depth, following traditionally in one pathway. Nick Sousanis voices that we fail to view things in different ways and want individuals to evolve out of their shell and expand on the perspective that they are used to. I believe flatness is the reduction of options that are presented to us, limiting us to a single perspective. Flat thinking is continuously perpetuated when we simply accept what we are told about the world. The people within the cave see the shadow figure presented to them in a one-dimension way. Living in the cave for decades with nothing other than darkness and one another, they began viewing the shadow as the gods, worshipping it, and not expanding their view any further. They view it as one, definite entity and do not consider any other possibilities related to the shadow or its origins.

2. Seeing Double

Beyond the cave are creatures known as Titians. In order for these creatures to live, they must absorb energy over a span of months. However, their precious possession has gone missing from its sacred place and is now isolated and floating in an unknown abyss. Outside of the cave is where the titan's source of life is; a colorful, dazzling diamond, hovering in the sky. With the sun so warm and brightly positioned behind the ravishing diamond, its sunlight beams at the object, casting a shadow into a tiny crevasse in the cave. What the cave people thought was the gods coming closer to them or the end of the world, was just the sun moving up and down, indicating it is either nighttime. With the different positioning of the sun affecting how the shadows are shown, the events and processes of the shadow are what the people in the cave would have never imagined as they have no idea what the world is like outside of their comfort zone within the cave.

In essence, Nick Sousanis' concept of "seeing double" encourages individuals to have a mindset that allows them to seek out different perspectives on the world. They can see through more than a single lens and can recognize and engage with the interconnectedness of ideas, experiences, and viewpoints. I believe “seeing double” is about being open to different alternatives, interpretations and insights, understanding that there are often multiple valid ways of understanding any given concept or phenomenon. The cave people have always lived viewing one perspective and do not see this shadow in any other perspective except their own, which is a sign from the gods whenever they are close to him or straying away from him by the shadow figure. However, their whole idea and view on things will soon change. In the middle of the night, one of the cave people that go by the name, Luey, climbs up and peaks outside of the cave through the little crevasse that the shadow is being cast through, curious at what really is out there but is afraid to doubt the gods that they all worship. Suddenly, rocks start to fall, making the tiny crevasses become big enough for Luey to squeeze into. He finally makes it out of the cave and is faced with the truth. He sees the same thing that he saw in the cave, except now it has more dimension and is colorful with a light source cast behind it. He turns around and sees his own figure just as strange captivating object in the sky. By putting two and two together, he understands that what he has been seeing this whole time was not a sign from the gods, but a dark and dull shadowed reflection of a the mesmerizing object within the sky. He runs back to the cave quickly notifying the rest of the people that live in the cave about his new discovery, showing them a new perspective of the world outside of the one they live in.
